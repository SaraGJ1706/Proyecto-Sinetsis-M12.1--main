Who we are?

SAE W is a non-profit NGO that aims to raise awareness about the harm caused by throwing plastics into the ocean. These plastics cause serious damage to marine fauna and flora, and in turn, they also harm us, since the fish we buy in supermarkets, before being weighed and packaged, ingest microplastics that we indirectly end up eating. Besides harming us, we inevitably destroy the fauna and flora, causing large amounts of marine litter, which forms large islands of waste that don't decompose for years.

Reflection

This is why the NGO was founded: to raise awareness and try to clean up beach debris with the help of others. People can help by donating, shopping at the charity shop in the donation section, or signing up for the cleanup activities we propose. All of this is aimed at having cleaner oceans and beaches and stopping the killing of animal species by polluting their natural habitats.

To fund our business, we have a shop selling personalized products featuring the NGO's image. 100% of the profits go toward continuing to grow as an organization and funding our events and aiding in the elimination of plastic. Feel free to come to our free collection and/or awareness events, because all we need is the will and support of the public to solve this huge problem that surrounds us. Join us!


Data provided by professionals

"The increase in plastic consumption, especially single-use plastics, is alarming. The production of bottles, cups, and food packaging contributes to visible pollution in rivers and lakes, threatening both the environment and human health."

-Ana Boischio, PAHO Regional Advisor on Chemical Safety-

"These substances can also affect people through inhalation. They can be released through tire abrasion, the wear and tear of synthetic textiles, garbage burning, and other processes. Their impact on human well-being will depend on the degree and type of exposure, the route of entry into the body, and also on factors such as health status, nutrition, tobacco use, among others."

-Luis Francisco Sánchez, PAHO Regional Advisor on Chemical Safety-
